Introduction

Hello, I'm Tina. I've been playing rhythm games of all kinds for some years. I actively play beatmania IIDX, but I also play a lot of others, namely SOUND VOLTEX, Dance Dance Revolution, Pump It Up, NotITG, pop'n music, Project Diva, Taiko no Tatsujin, and the list goes on...

osu! was the first rhythm game I played actively. I was launching the game frequently, trying to improve myself, and trying to climb in the leaderboard. At some point, it made me aware of other existing rhythm games, and I started investing time and money in them instead of osu!.

I still like osu! a lot, it certainly made something interesting out of Ouendan's gameplay. mania also opened a lot of persons to traditional rhythm games.

However, there are a lot of things that feel wrong to me in osu!, and I want to fix them. With osmosis, I'm trying to fix all the things I don't like about osu! in the form of an alternative ranking system.

You can get involved with Project osmosis right now by joining the Discord server or by contributing to the public code repository on GitHub.

I want to emphasize on the fact that I'm not trying to change the face of osu!. It has its own online ranking and my goal is absolutely not to replace it. What I want is to give an option for players who want something different than what is currently in place.
osu! has its own way of doing things and it's enough for most players. But part of its players are frustrated by the current system and want something different. I'm working for those people.

What is wrong with osu!?

The scoring system in standard is super wrong. The combo weights all of the score. Even though combo rewards consistency of players, rhythm games are about playing a song correctly. This can mostly be judged by the ability of the player to keep a given accuracy no matter the difficulty of the patterns to execute.
Different ways to reward consistency can be to give a bonus if the players fullcombo some parts. This is can be seen in Project Diva for example. In osu!, that would be fullcombo-ing Kiai parts. Another way would be to give a bonus at the end depending on the max combo obtained.

The ranked beatmaps is an hell of copyright infrigement. Rhythm games elegantly ties together the art of making games and making music. Both parties have to be respected for a rhythm game to grow peacefully in the ecosystem. Balantly infringing copyright will only makes you lose esteem from composers and makes music licensing companies able to sue you. Respecting artists rights actually usually encourages more artists to get interested in your game, opening more opportunities for new legitimate original content.

The pp system sucks and Score V2 isn't going to fix it.

How could Project osmosis fix that?

Imagine a leaderboard where scores and pp are based on your accuracy and only legitimate songs are considered for being added to the ranked pool. Welcome to Project osmosis.

Score is purely based on accuracy. pp are given depending on your accuracy on a set amount of highest scores. Beatmaps are carefully reviewed before being added to the ranking pool and can only be added if using them is authorized by their authors. Songs from osu! featured artists take priority.

How does osmosis work?

Scores you do on the official client are sent to osu! servers, osmosis keep a copy of these scores and if they match its ranking criteria, it will use it for pp calculation / leaderboards.

Why not making a seperate game?

Maintaining an online game is a very tedious task. Left aside the considerable effort of creating an entire video game. I don't consider having the game dev and managing skills to make a game like osu!, including online features, and then maintaining it.

If you want to help, you can mod the current osu! or the open-source one to make things easier for osmosis. Namely sending custom score data to osmosis server on song completion in addition or instead of sending to osu! servers would be a tremendous help.

Features

  • Per-map leaderboard per accuracy: Compete on ranked maps using accuracy as the strong metric instead of score.
  • Global leaderboard with original pp system: Compete with others for the first place with a pp system that rewards accuracy.
  • Ethically correct ranking criterias: Why stealing songs when thousands composed by recognized artists can be enjoyed legally? We don't support osu!'s passive take on copyright infrigement that waits for the companies to DMCA them. Lots of artists don't make the effort and some get hurts by being associated with osu!. The fact that some other artists are ok with it doesn't excuse the fact that others were hurt.
  • Custom manually set Star Rating: Star Rating plays an important role in the pp system, algorithmically determined SR is inefficient and inacurate.
